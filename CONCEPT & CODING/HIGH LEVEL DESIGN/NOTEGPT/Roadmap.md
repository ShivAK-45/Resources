### Summary of Video Content: System Design Roadmap by Shreyash Jain

This video by Shreyash Jain provides a detailed **roadmap and future plan** for mastering **Low-Level Design (LLD)** and **High-Level Design (HLD)**, focusing primarily on interview preparation for system design roles. The content is structured around the prerequisites, teaching methodology, covered topics, upcoming videos, and additional announcements relevant to learners.

---

### Core Concepts and Roadmap Overview

#### Low-Level Design (LLD)

- **Prerequisites:**
    - Strong understanding of **Object-Oriented Programming (OOP)** fundamentals: inheritance, polymorphism, abstraction.
    - Familiarity with any OOP language such as C++, Java, or Python.
    - Mastery of **SOLID principles**, which form the foundational design rules.

- **Design Patterns:**
    - There are 23 design patterns in total.
    - The channel has covered several **important design patterns** (indicated by green ticks).
    - The teaching approach is to cover a **few key design patterns first**, then solve interview questions applying these patterns.
    - Remaining design patterns and related questions will be covered sequentially.

- **Interview Questions Covered:**
    - These questions are sourced from **actual product-based company interviews**.
    - Examples of covered problems:
        - Notify-me system
        - Pizza billing system
        - Parking lot
        - Snake and ladder game
        - Elevator system
        - Car rental system
        - Logging system
        - Tic-tac-toe game
        - BookMyShow
        - Vending machine
        - ATM
        - Chess game
        - File system
        - Splitwise (marked as very important)

- **Upcoming Focus:**
    - Implementation of **Splitwise simplify algorithm** (important for both LLD and DSA interviews).
    - Other pending questions like Cricbuzz and more, with sequencing subject to priority and user feedback.

- **Teaching Style:**
    - No rigid chapter structure or coding-heavy sessions as in HLD.
    - Emphasis on **conceptual clarity and interview applicability**.
    - No single “correct” solution; encourages learning through structured, sequential content.

---

#### High-Level Design (HLD)

- **Key Differences from LLD:**
    - Requires prior knowledge of **distributed systems concepts** before solving questions.
    - Larger, more complex questions often require foundational understanding of technologies and patterns.

- **Covered Topics:**
    - Network protocols and architectures: **TCP, WebSocket, HTTP, WebRTC, client-server, peer-to-peer**.
    - Consistency and reliability: **CAP theorem**.
    - Microservices and design patterns: emphasis on **Saga pattern** (critical) and mention of **Strangler pattern** (optional).
    - Scaling systems: **scale from zero to millions of users**.
    - Data structures and storage: **consistent hashing, DynamoDB, key-value stores**.
    - Database concepts: **SQL vs NoSQL**, indexing, partitioning, mirroring, leader election.
    - Estimation techniques: **back-of-the-envelope calculations** for resource planning.
    - Rate limiting and other system components.

- **Upcoming HLD Topics:**
    - Autocomplete system (also called iPad system).
    - Advanced system components: **Kafka, queues, proxies, CDN, storage types (block, file, S3, RAID), Bloom filters, Merkle trees, Gaussian protocols, caching**.
    - Scaling databases: horizontal/vertical scaling, partitioning strategies.

- **Teaching Approach:**
    - Sequential topic coverage followed by solving related interview questions.
    - Focus on **conceptual understanding and practical system design skills**.
    - No fixed timeline; content release depends on bandwidth and priority.
